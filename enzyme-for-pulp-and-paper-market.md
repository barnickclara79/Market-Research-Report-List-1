<p><p>The enzyme market for pulp and paper includes various types, each serving specific functions. Amylase aids in starch degradation, improving paper quality and reducing stickiness. Cellulase breaks down cellulose, enhancing fiber accessibility and refining pulping processes. Xylanase targets hemicellulose, leading to better extraction and reduced bleaching chemicals. Lipase assists in removing fats and oils, promoting cleaner processes. Other enzymes, such as laccase and peroxidase, support broader applications like biobleaching and wastewater treatment, optimizing overall efficiency in pulp and paper production.</p></p>
