<p><p>The industrial next-generation refrigerants market features two primary types: natural refrigerants and hydrofluoroolefins (HFOs). Natural refrigerants include substances like ammonia, carbon dioxide, and hydrocarbons, known for their low environmental impact and minimal ozone depletion potential. In contrast, hydrofluoroolefins are synthetic compounds designed to replace traditional hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), offering lower global warming potential while maintaining efficiency. Together, these refrigerants support industries in meeting stringent environmental regulations and transitioning to more sustainable cooling solutions.</p></p>
