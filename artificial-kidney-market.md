<p><p>The artificial kidney market includes two primary types: wearable and implantable artificial kidneys. Wearable artificial kidneys are portable devices designed for patients to use outside of clinical settings, allowing for greater mobility and flexibility in managing kidney function. In contrast, implantable artificial kidneys are surgically placed inside the body, providing continuous kidney function and reducing the need for dialysis sessions. Both types aim to enhance the quality of life for patients with renal failure while addressing the limitations of traditional dialysis methods.</p></p>
