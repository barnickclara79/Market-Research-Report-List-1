<p><p>Floorstanding speakers are typically categorized into two main market types: active and passive speakers. Active speakers contain built-in amplifiers, allowing for direct connection to audio sources, making them convenient for users seeking simplified setups. They often include onboard processing for enhanced sound quality. Conversely, passive speakers require an external amplifier to function, offering greater flexibility in customizing audio systems. This distinction influences consumer choices based on preferences for sound quality, setup complexity, and space considerations in home or professional environments.</p></p>
