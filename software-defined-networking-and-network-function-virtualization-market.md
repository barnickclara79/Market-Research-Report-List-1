<p><p>Software-Defined Networking (SDN) and Network Function Virtualization (NFV) are transformative technologies that optimize how networks operate and deliver services. SDN decouples the control plane from the data plane, allowing centralized management and programmable network resources to enhance flexibility and innovation. NFV complements this by virtualizing entire classes of network services, allowing traditional hardware appliances to be replaced with software running on commodity servers, which promotes scalability and cost-efficiency.</p><p>The Software-Defined Networking and Network Function Virtualization Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.9% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the increasing need for agile network infrastructure, the rising adoption of cloud-based services, and the demand for low-latency connectivity. Additionally, enterprises are increasingly recognizing the value of automation and orchestration in network management, which is fueling market expansion. </p><p>Key trends include the integration of artificial intelligence in network management, the shift towards edge computing, and the convergence of SDN and NFV technologies to support 5G deployments.
