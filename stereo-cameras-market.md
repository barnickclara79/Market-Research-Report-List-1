<p><ul><li>Time-of-flight</li><li>Stereo Vision</li><li>Structured Light</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The stereo cameras market comprises three main types: time-of-flight, stereo vision, and structured light. Time-of-flight cameras measure distance by capturing the time it takes for emitted light to return, enabling precise depth perception. Stereo vision systems use two or more cameras to simulate human binocular vision, calculating depth through disparity between images. Structured light cameras project a known pattern onto a scene and analyze deformations to gauge depth, making them effective for 3D scanning and object recognition.</p></p>
