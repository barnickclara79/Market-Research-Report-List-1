<p><p>The barcoding software market is segmented into three key types: one-dimensional (1D) barcoding, two-dimensional (2D) barcoding, and 3D barcoding. 1D barcodes, consisting of parallel lines, are widely used for basic inventory tracking. 2D barcodes, which can store more data in a compact format, are prevalent in applications like ticketing and QR codes. 3D barcoding incorporates advanced technologies, such as imaging or digital encoding, enhancing data capacity and enabling complex applications like product authentication and tracking in supply chains.</p></p>
