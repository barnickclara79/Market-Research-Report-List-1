<p><p>The automotive high-strength steel (HSS) market is divided into two main types: conventional HSS and advanced high-strength steel (AHSS). Conventional HSS is designed to enhance structural integrity while maintaining lightweight properties, primarily using standard alloys and processing techniques. In contrast, AHSS incorporates complex alloys and innovative manufacturing processes, offering superior strength-to-weight ratios and enhanced performance characteristics. Both types are essential for improving vehicle safety, fuel efficiency, and compliance with increasingly stringent automotive regulations.</p></p>
