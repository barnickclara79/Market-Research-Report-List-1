<p><p>The self-propelled barges market is divided into two primary types: inland and ocean barges. Inland barges are designed for transportation along rivers and lakes, featuring shallow drafts and robust structures suited for navigating narrow waterways. They primarily carry bulk commodities and goods over shorter distances. Ocean barges, on the other hand, are built for maritime navigation, enabling the transport of goods across oceans. These vessels are equipped to handle larger loads and are utilized for longer-haul shipping routes.</p></p>
