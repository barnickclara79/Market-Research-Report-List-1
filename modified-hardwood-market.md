<p><p>Modified hardwood market types encompass various processes aimed at enhancing wood properties for durability and performance. Thermal treatment involves exposing wood to high temperatures to improve resistance to decay and insects while minimizing moisture absorption. Acetylation chemically alters wood by reacting it with acetic anhydride, resulting in increased dimensional stability and resistance to environmental degradation. Other treatments may include chemicals or biological processes to further enhance wood's properties, offering sustainable options for construction and furniture industries.</p></p>
