<p><p>Degradable Polyhydroxyalkanoate (PHA) refers to a group of biopolymers produced by microbial fermentation, known for their biodegradability and versatility in applications. The key types include PHB (Polyhydroxybutyrate), recognized for its crystallinity and strength, PHBV (Polyhydroxybutyrate-co-valerate), which offers enhanced flexibility, and PHBHx (Polyhydroxybutyrate-co-hexanoate), ideal for applications needing elasticity. PHB4B is a variant designed for particular performance characteristics. Other PHAs include blends and copolymers tailored for specific industrial needs, contributing to sustainable packaging and biomedical products.</p></p>
