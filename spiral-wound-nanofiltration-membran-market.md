<p><p>The spiral wound nanofiltration membrane market comprises two primary types: composite membranes and asymmetric membranes. Composite membranes feature a thin, selective outer layer supported by a thicker, porous substrate, allowing for high permeability and selectivity. Asymmetric membranes, on the other hand, have a gradual transition in pore size, enhancing structural integrity. Both types are used in various applications, including water treatment, food and beverage processing, and wastewater management, due to their efficiency in removing contaminants while allowing desirable molecules to pass through.</p></p>
